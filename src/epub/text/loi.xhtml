List of Illustrations

  1. The King and Queen of Hearts were seated on their throne, with a great crowd assembled about them⁠—all sorts of little birds and beasts, as well as the whole pack of cards.

  2. When the Rabbit actually took a watch out of its waistcoat-pocket, and looked at it, and then hurried on, Alice started to her feet.

  3. She came upon a low curtain she had not noticed before, and behind it was a little door about fifteen inches high.

  4. Round the neck of the bottle was a paper label, with the words “Drink Me” beautifully printed on it in large letters.

  5. “Now I’m opening out like the largest telescope that ever was! Goodbye, feet!”

  6. The Rabbit skurried away into the darkness as hard as he could go.

  7. She was in the pool of tears which she had wept when she was nine feet high.

  8. The Mouse was swimming away from her as hard as it could go, and making quite a commotion in the pool as it went.

  9. They were indeed a queer-looking party that assembled on the bank.

  10. They all crowded round her once more, while the Dodo solemnly presented the thimble.

  11. She tried the effect of lying down with one elbow against the door, and the other arm curled round her head.

  12. She did not get hold of anything, but she heard a little shriek and a fall, and a crash of broken glass.

  13. The first thing she heard was a general chorus of “There goes Bill!”

  14. An enormous puppy was looking down at her with large round eyes.

  15. The Caterpillar and Alice looked at each other for some time in silence.

  16. Yet you turned a back-somersault in at the door.

  17. Yet you finished the goose, with the bones and the beak.

  18. Yet you balanced an eel on the end of your nose.

  19. The Fish-Footman began by producing from under his arm a great letter.

  20. The Duchess was sitting on a three-legged stool in the middle, nursing a baby.

  21. It was neither more nor less than a pig, and she felt that it would be quite absurd for her to carry it further.

  22. She was a little startled by seeing the Cheshire Cat sitting on a bough of a tree a few yards off.

  23. This time it vanished quite slowly, beginning with the end of the tail, and ending with the grin.

  24. The Hatter opened his eyes very wide on hearing this.

  25. Twinkle, twinkle, little bat!

  26. The last time she saw them, they were trying to put the Dormouse into the teapot.

  27. The roses were white, but there were three gardeners busily painting them red.

  28. The Queen turned crimson with fury, and screamed “Off with her head!”

  29. The chief difficulty Alice found at first was in managing her flamingo.

  30. When she got back to the Cheshire Cat, she was surprised to find quite a large crowd collected round it.

  31. She was exactly the right height to rest her chin upon Alice’s shoulder, and it was an uncomfortably sharp chin.

  32. They very soon came upon a Gryphon, lying fast asleep in the sun.

  33. “When we were little,” the Mock Turtle went on at last, more calmly, “we went to school in the sea.”

  34. So they began solemnly dancing round and round Alice, every now and then treading on her toes.

  35. Her head was so full of the Lobster Quadrille, that she hardly knew what she was saying.

  36. The White Rabbit blew three blasts on the trumpet.

  37. “I’m a poor man, your Majesty,” the Hatter began, in a trembling voice.

  38. “I’d rather finish my tea,” said the Hatter, with an anxious look at the Queen.

  39. She jumped up in such a hurry that she tipped over the jury-box with the edge of her skirt.

  40. “Why, there they are!” said the King triumphantly, pointing to the tarts on the table.

  41. At this the whole pack rose up into the air, and came flying down upon her.
